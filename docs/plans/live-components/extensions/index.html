Two sample extensions

Ship two extensions in the MVP — a richer one (todo-list) and a trivial one (counter). Two kinds in one chat forces the registry to dispatch by kind; the two together prove the manifest contract scales both ways.

Requirements

  1. Both extensions can be active in the same chat session at the same time, each with its own doc, without any code in the chat core touching either kind.
  2. The reader, after seeing both, can predict how a third extension (kanban, calendar) would be shaped without further documentation.

Why two and not one

A single sample extension proves the manifest contract works for one shape, but it can't prove the contract is generic. Two extensions force the chat core to demonstrate three properties:

  • The registry resolves the right manifest by kind on every render.
  • Two doc stores don't bleed state into each other.
  • The wire envelope (data-live-op) correctly tags ops with kind so the reducer dispatch picks the right reducer.

Picking one mutation-heavy extension and one trivial extension is deliberate: it reveals whether the contract is too verbose for simple cases (a counter shouldn't need a Zod schema with 10 lines of boilerplate) or too loose for complex ones (a todo list with edit-in-place needs more than just "increment").
